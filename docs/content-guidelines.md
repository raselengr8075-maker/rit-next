# RIT content guidelines

## Accuracy first

Do not invent or imply clients, partners, awards, certifications, qualifications, research results, performance, prices, capacities, downloads, ratings, employees, countries, testimonials, addresses, phone numbers, business hours, or social accounts. If a fact is not verified, omit it or say that verified content will be added later.

Use precise development labels: `planned`, `concept`, `research`, `development`, `testing`, `prototype`, `active`, or `available` only when the owner confirms the status. Never describe unfinished work as validated or commercially available.

## Bilingual writing

Write English and Bangla independently; do not use runtime machine translation. Preserve product names. Keep headings short, summaries factual, and the two languages equivalent in meaning. Review Bangla spelling and punctuation before publishing.

## Images and documents

- Upload only JPEG, PNG, or WebP images you are authorized to use; maximum 5 MB.
- Prefer WebP, meaningful alternative text, and approximately 1600×1000 for hero media or 1000×750 for cards.
- Do not put essential text inside images.
- Check PDFs for readable text, document title, language, authorship, file size, and accessibility before adding them to Resources.
- Partner logos and portraits require permission and verified identity.

## News and SEO

State what happened and when. Separate expected outcomes from verified outcomes. SEO titles should be specific and about 50–60 characters; descriptions should summarize the actual page in roughly 120–160 characters.
